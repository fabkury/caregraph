- MSPB (Medicare Spending Per Beneficiary) captures total Part A and Part B spending from 3 days prior to admission through 30 days post-discharge, providing a comprehensive episode-of-care cost picture that includes post-acute care (SNF, home health, rehab) — hospitals with aggressive discharge to expensive post-acute settings will show higher MSPB even if inpatient costs are low.
- The measure is price-standardized to remove geographic wage index differences, so it isolates utilization intensity rather than regional price variation. However, it does not adjust for case mix beyond the DRG assignment.
- MSPB is expressed as a ratio to the national median episode cost — a value of 1.0 means spending equals the national median, above 1.0 means higher than median. Most hospitals cluster between 0.85 and 1.15.
- The spending window captures costs billed by all providers during the episode, not just the index hospital — spending by skilled nursing facilities, home health agencies, and outpatient providers is attributed to the hospital that performed the index admission.
