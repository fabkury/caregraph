- Unplanned hospital visit measures include ED visits after hospital outpatient surgery (OP-36) and unplanned readmissions after outpatient procedures — these complement the HRRP inpatient readmission measures by capturing outpatient-to-ED and outpatient-to-inpatient transitions.
- Observation stays (outpatient status) are counted differently than inpatient admissions, and whether a return visit is classified as "ED visit" vs. "readmission" depends on the patient's admission status, not the clinical severity of the event.
- Hospitals that perform mostly low-risk outpatient procedures will naturally have lower return rates than hospitals performing complex outpatient surgeries, but the risk adjustment does not fully account for procedure-level complexity differences.
- The measures use claims-based identification of "unplanned" visits, which relies on CMS's planned readmission algorithm — some clinically planned follow-ups may be misclassified as unplanned if the coding does not match the algorithm's expected patterns.
