- This dataset reports the prevalence of 21 chronic conditions (e.g., diabetes, hypertension, heart failure, COPD, Alzheimer's/dementia, depression, cancer) among Medicare FFS beneficiaries by county, based on claims-derived condition flags using the CMS Chronic Conditions Data Warehouse (CCW) algorithms.
- Prevalence rates reflect Medicare fee-for-service beneficiaries only. Medicare Advantage enrollees are excluded, which can significantly bias prevalence estimates in counties with high MA penetration (>50% in many markets). In these counties, the FFS population may be systematically different from the overall Medicare population.
- Conditions are identified from claims using CCW reference period algorithms that require a specific number of qualifying claims within a lookback window (1-2 years depending on the condition). Under-coding and under-diagnosis cause prevalence to be underestimated, particularly for conditions like depression and Alzheimer's where diagnostic coding is inconsistent.
- Counties with fewer than 11 beneficiaries with a given condition are suppressed. This primarily affects small rural counties and less-common conditions (e.g., HIV/AIDS, hepatitis).
- Multi-morbidity is the norm in this population: over 60% of Medicare FFS beneficiaries have 2+ chronic conditions. The individual condition prevalence rates are not mutually exclusive — the same beneficiary is counted in multiple conditions.
