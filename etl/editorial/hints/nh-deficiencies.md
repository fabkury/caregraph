- Deficiencies are cited during state survey agency inspections conducted on behalf of CMS. Standard health inspections occur on a 9-15 month cycle (target: every 12 months), but complaint investigations can occur at any time and generate additional deficiency citations.
- Each deficiency is classified by scope (isolated, pattern, widespread) and severity (no actual harm with potential for minimal harm, up to immediate jeopardy to resident health or safety). The combination produces a letter code from A to L on the CMS scope/severity grid. Only deficiencies at level G or above (actual harm or immediate jeopardy) trigger automatic enforcement actions.
- Deficiency counts are heavily influenced by state survey agency practices. Some states cite more deficiencies per inspection on average than others — cross-state comparisons of raw deficiency counts are unreliable without adjusting for state surveyor behavior.
- The F-tag (deficiency tag number) identifies the specific federal regulation violated (e.g., F-tag 0689 = "Free of Accident Hazards/Supervision/Devices"). The F-tag numbering was completely revised in November 2017 (the "mega-rule"), so historical comparisons across this boundary require tag-mapping crosswalks.
- Facilities under a "Special Focus Facility" (SFF) designation receive more frequent inspections (every 6 months), generating more deficiency records per year than non-SFF facilities even at equivalent quality levels.
