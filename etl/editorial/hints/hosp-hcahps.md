- HCAHPS (Hospital Consumer Assessment of Healthcare Providers and Systems) is a standardized patient experience survey administered 48 hours to 6 weeks after discharge — it measures patient perception, not clinical quality, and the two can diverge significantly.
- Results are adjusted for patient mix (age, education, self-rated health, primary language, service line) using CMS's mode-and-patient-mix adjustment, but hospitals with predominantly sicker or lower-health-literacy populations may still show systematically lower scores.
- The survey has a "top-box" scoring methodology: only responses of "Always" (on a 4-point scale) or "9" or "10" (on a 0-10 scale) count as top-box. This means the difference between "Usually" and "Always" is treated as a quality gap, which may not reflect meaningful differences in care.
- Survey response rates have declined steadily (national average ~25%) and vary dramatically by hospital (15-45%); low response rates introduce non-response bias that the patient-mix adjustment does not fully correct.
- Star ratings are derived from HCAHPS scores using a clustering algorithm, not fixed thresholds — a hospital's star rating can change even if its raw scores do not, if the national distribution shifts.
