- ACO participant lists identify the TINs (Tax Identification Numbers) and, where applicable, CCNs (CMS Certification Numbers) of providers participating in each MSSP ACO. A single TIN may represent hundreds of individual clinicians in a large group practice — the participant count does not equal the provider count.
- Participant lists are published as a point-in-time snapshot, typically reflecting the roster as of January 1 of the performance year. Mid-year additions (via ACO expansion) and departures are not captured until the next annual file.
- Hospital participants are identified by CCN, enabling cross-linking to hospital entity pages. However, not all ACO participants are hospitals — physician group practices, FQHCs, and other institutional providers also participate but may lack a CCN.
- Some providers participate in multiple ACOs across different TINs (e.g., a physician group with locations in two ACO service areas). This is permitted under MSSP rules but can create double-counting if not handled carefully in cross-link analysis.
