- CDC SDOH (Social Determinants of Health) measures compile county-level indicators from the American Community Survey (ACS), Census Bureau, USDA, and other federal sources covering economic stability, education, healthcare access, neighborhood environment, and social/community context.
- Most measures use ACS 5-year estimates, which smooth short-term fluctuations but can mask rapid changes (e.g., economic shocks, pandemic impacts). Small-population counties rely entirely on 5-year estimates, which carry wider margins of error than 1-year estimates available for larger counties.
- Temporal alignment with health outcome data is imperfect: SDOH measures may use ACS data from a different year than the CMS or CDC health data they are paired with on CareGraph county pages. For example, 2020 ACS data (affected by Census pandemic disruptions) may be paired with 2023 health outcomes.
- The selection of SDOH indicators is based on the CDC's Healthy People 2030 framework and does not include all possible social determinants. Notable omissions may include granular housing quality data, environmental pollution indices, and local policy variables.
- County-level aggregation masks substantial within-county variation, particularly in large or diverse counties where census tract-level SDOH conditions may range from very advantaged to very disadvantaged.
