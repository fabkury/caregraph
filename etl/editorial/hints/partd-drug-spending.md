- Medicare Part D Spending by Drug reports total spending, claim counts, beneficiary counts, and average cost per dosage unit for drugs covered under Medicare Part D (primarily outpatient/retail prescription drugs). It does not include Part B physician-administered drugs.
- Drugs are reported by brand name and generic name. CareGraph aggregates by generic name (uppercased) to present a unified drug page — this merges all brand-name formulations, manufacturers, and dosage forms under one generic, which may obscure significant price differences between branded and generic versions.
- Drugs with fewer than 11 claims or 11 beneficiaries in the reporting year are excluded entirely to protect patient privacy. This disproportionately removes rare disease drugs, newly launched drugs, and drugs nearing the end of their market lifecycle.
- "Total Spending" includes all payer components: plan-paid amounts, beneficiary cost-sharing, and low-income subsidy payments. It reflects gross spending before manufacturer rebates, which are not publicly disclosed. True net spending is substantially lower for rebate-heavy drugs.
- Annual changes in spending can reflect price increases, utilization changes, generic entry, formulary shifts, or changes in the Part D benefit structure (e.g., the Inflation Reduction Act price negotiation program starting 2026) — the dataset does not decompose spending changes by driver.
