- NADAC (National Average Drug Acquisition Cost) is a Medicaid program, not Medicare. It reflects the average price that retail community pharmacies pay to acquire drugs, based on a voluntary monthly survey of ~2,500 pharmacies conducted by Myers and Stauffer for CMS.
- NADAC is the most transparent publicly available measure of actual drug acquisition costs in the US. However, it reflects Medicaid-channel pharmacy acquisition costs — prices in other channels (hospital, specialty, mail-order, 340B) may differ substantially.
- Survey response rates vary by drug. For widely dispensed generics, NADAC is based on robust survey data. For specialty drugs or drugs with limited retail distribution, the survey may have few respondents, producing less reliable estimates.
- NADAC reflects ingredient cost only — it does not include dispensing fees, pharmacy markups, or manufacturer rebates. The gap between NADAC and what payers actually pay (net of rebates) can be substantial for branded drugs.
- NADAC is updated weekly by CMS. CareGraph uses the most recent available snapshot at the time of ETL. Historical NADAC data is available but CareGraph currently captures only the point-in-time price, not price trends.
- Matching NADAC records to CareGraph drug entities requires joining on drug name, which can be ambiguous for combination products, extended-release formulations, or drugs with multiple salt forms.
