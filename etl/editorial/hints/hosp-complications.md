- Mortality rates (MORT-30 measures) and complication rates are risk-adjusted using CMS hierarchical logistic regression models that adjust for age, sex, and clinical comorbidities from claims data — but do not adjust for socioeconomic status, race, or hospital characteristics.
- The 3-year measurement window (e.g., July 2019 through June 2022 for FY2026) overlaps with the COVID-19 pandemic; CMS excluded certain discharge periods but the remaining data may still reflect pandemic-era operational disruptions.
- "Better than the National Rate," "No Different than the National Rate," and "Worse than the National Rate" categories are determined by whether the 95% confidence interval for the hospital's rate overlaps the national rate — hospitals with small volumes will almost always fall into "No Different" due to wide confidence intervals.
- PSI-90 (Patient Safety and Adverse Events Composite) is a composite of multiple patient safety indicators; the composite score can mask deterioration in individual component measures.
- Complication measures are derived from claims data, not chart review — coding practices vary across hospitals and can affect reported rates independently of actual complication frequency.
