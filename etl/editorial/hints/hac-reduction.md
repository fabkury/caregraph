- The HAC Reduction Program imposes a 1% payment reduction on hospitals scoring in the worst-performing quartile on a composite of patient safety measures. The penalty is binary (bottom 25% or not) with no graduated scale — a hospital barely in the 25th percentile receives the same penalty as one in the 1st percentile.
- The Total HAC Score is a weighted composite of two measure domains: CMS Patient Safety Indicators (PSI-90, claims-based) and CDC NHSN Healthcare-Associated Infection measures (CLABSI, CAUTI, SSI colon, SSI hysterectomy, MRSA, CDI). The weighting between domains has changed across fiscal years.
- Critical access hospitals, hospitals with fewer than 200 discharges, and Maryland waiver hospitals are excluded from the program. Specialty hospitals (psychiatric, rehabilitation, children's, cancer) paid under non-IPPS systems are also excluded.
- There is significant overlap with measures used in the Hospital Value-Based Purchasing program (safety domain) and the Hospital-Acquired Condition measures on Hospital Compare — the same hospital can be penalized or rewarded for overlapping indicators under different programs.
- Safety-net hospitals and hospitals serving high-acuity populations have argued the program disproportionately penalizes them because risk adjustment does not fully account for social determinants of health.
