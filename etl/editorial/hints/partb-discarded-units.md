- The Part B Discarded Drug Units dataset tracks waste from single-dose vials of physician-administered drugs. CMS requires providers to bill the JW modifier (indicating discarded drug) and, since 2023, the JZ modifier (indicating no discarded drug) to improve waste reporting completeness.
- Drug waste is primarily driven by vial size mismatch: if a drug comes in a 100mg vial but the patient's dose is 60mg, 40mg is discarded and billed with the JW modifier. Drugs with limited vial size options relative to common dosing regimens show the highest discard percentages.
- Compliance with JW/JZ modifier reporting is imperfect. Facilities that fail to append the JW modifier when discarding drug will not appear in this dataset — absence of records may indicate non-reporting rather than zero waste.
- The dataset is used by CMS to calculate refunds under the Inflation Reduction Act Section 11101 (discarded drug refund program), which requires manufacturers to refund Medicare for discarded amounts exceeding a threshold percentage. This creates a financial incentive for manufacturers to offer smaller vial sizes.
- Discarded amounts represent the billing-unit equivalent, not physical volume. The financial impact depends on the drug's ASP — high-cost biologics with high discard rates represent substantial waste spending even if the physical volume discarded is small.
