- Medicare Part B drug spending covers physician-administered drugs (infusions, injections, inhalation drugs given in office or hospital outpatient settings) billed under HCPCS J-codes and Q-codes. Oral and self-administered drugs are generally excluded — they fall under Part D.
- Reimbursement for Part B drugs is based on the Average Sales Price (ASP) plus a percentage markup (currently ASP+6% in non-sequestration years). The "average spending per dosage unit" in this dataset reflects this ASP-based reimbursement, not the drug's actual acquisition cost or list price.
- HCPCS codes may map to multiple generic drugs or drug combinations (e.g., a single J-code for "not otherwise classified" chemotherapy agents). This complicates the join to Part D drug entities by generic name and may result in some drugs appearing under umbrella categories.
- Part B drug spending is heavily concentrated in a small number of high-cost categories: oncology, ophthalmology (anti-VEGF agents), rheumatology (biologics), and immunology. The top 20 drugs by spending account for the majority of total Part B drug expenditures.
- Biosimilar uptake is visible in this dataset as brand-name biologic spending declines and biosimilar HCPCS codes gain volume, but the pace of biosimilar adoption varies dramatically by therapeutic area and is influenced by provider reimbursement incentives.
