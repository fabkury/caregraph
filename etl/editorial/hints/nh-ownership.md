- Nursing home ownership is self-reported on CMS Form 855A (Medicare enrollment application) and includes the facility's legal entity, managing entity, and individuals or organizations with 5% or more ownership interest.
- Complex ownership structures (private equity firms, real estate investment trusts, multi-layered holding companies) may not be fully transparent in this dataset. The "operating company" listed may differ from the ultimate beneficial owner — CMS has proposed but not yet finalized rules requiring more granular ownership disclosure.
- The dataset captures current ownership only. Ownership history and change-of-ownership (CHOW) events are not included, though CHOW events are significant because CMS resets certain enforcement actions upon ownership change, and quality often declines during ownership transitions.
- Chain affiliation is indicated but the definition of "chain" varies — some small regional operators with 2-3 facilities are classified as chains alongside national operators with 100+ facilities.
- For-profit, nonprofit, and government ownership categories are the most commonly used classification, but the distinction can be misleading: some nonprofit facilities are part of large health systems that behave similarly to for-profit chains in terms of staffing and investment patterns.
