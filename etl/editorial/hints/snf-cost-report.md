- SNF cost reports are filed on CMS Form 2540-10 (freestanding SNFs) or as part of CMS Form 2552-10 (hospital-based SNF units). The two forms have different worksheet structures, so field mappings differ between freestanding and hospital-based facilities.
- Key derived metrics (operating margin, cost per resident day, occupancy rate, Medicare/Medicaid day shares) must be calculated from raw worksheet line items. Occupancy rate is calculated from available bed-days and total patient days reported on Worksheet S-3.
- Medicaid day share is particularly important for SNFs because Medicaid typically reimburses well below cost. Facilities with high Medicaid day shares may show negative operating margins that reflect payer mix rather than operational inefficiency.
- Fiscal years are facility-defined and do not align across all SNFs. Comparing facilities on "the same year's" cost report can mix time periods by up to 11 months.
- Cost reports are self-reported and subject to CMS audit, but only a small fraction are audited annually. Common issues include misallocation of costs between nursing care and ancillary services, and inconsistent treatment of contracted services.
